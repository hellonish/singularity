As highlighted in the synthesis from reliable sources like Smith [Smith2024], DSP primarily focuses on processes such as sampling, which converts continuous signals into discrete data points, quantization, which approximates these points to a finite set of values, and filtering, which removes unwanted noise to enhance signal clarity. For instance, sampling adheres to the Nyquist theorem, which states that the sampling rate must be at least twice the highest frequency in the signal to avoid aliasing, a principle verified through consistent evidence in Jones [Jones2023].

Filtering techniques, such as low-pass or high-pass filters, then refine the signal by attenuating certain frequencies, as seen in applications where background noise is removed from audio recordings.
